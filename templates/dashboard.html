Accountability Court Data




Participants by Graduation Status

The majority of participants in Accountability Court programs graduate the program, saving the state approximately $22,000 per participant. A not insignificant amount do end the program by discharge (could be relocation, medical-related, or death) or termination (breaching program protocol, etc.).



Participants by Age

The most populated range of age of participants is the 30-39 range. The ages tend to reduce further out from that range, with an exceptional boost in the 55-59 age range. Expectedly, the smallest age range, is 0-17, considering many in this age range could also be redirected to programs for children.





Accountability Court Graduation Status by Primary Drug Choice

The two largest categories of drug choice are amphetamines and alcohol. Interestingly, while more program participants consider amphetamines their drug of choice, very slightly more alcohol-consuming participants graduated their programs. There is also a significant number less of alcohol-consuming participants that end their programs by termination.


Accountability Court Graduation Status by Education Level

This graph illustrates participant success status classified by their education level. The more educated, the higher success rate. The overall highest group, "high school graduate" also has the largest group of "terminated" participants. The "some college" group, while smaller, also has a smaller group of "terminated" participants.
